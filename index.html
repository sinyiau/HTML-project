Should Illinois be Alarmed by the First U.S. Human Death from Bird Flu?

By Sinyi Au

The U.S. has recorded its first human death from highly pathogenic avian influenza H5N1 infection, commonly known as bird flu. This marks a significant development in the ongoing bird flu situation, as it is the first fatal case among the 67 confirmed human infections in the U.S. since 2022.

Source: Bloomberg News

Recent outbreaks have been confirmed in commercial poultry operations in Delaware, California, and Michigan, as well as in backyard flocks in Arkansas and Idaho.

Although Illinois has not reported any human cases of H5N1 as of Jan. 13, 2025. However, it has seen a significant increase in bird deaths due to avian influenza. The Illinois Department of Natural Resources reported 132 avian flu-related bird deaths in the state over the last month.The state is implementing preventive measures, including increased biosecurity efforts on poultry farms.

  • Human H5N1 cases in the U.S. since 2024: 66
  • Number of states reporting human cases: 10
  • Number of states with infected dairy cattle herds: 16
  • Total infected dairy cattle herds: 924
  • Infected dairy cattle herds in California: 707
